Setup: Teams: Form teams of two players each. Each team consists of a "rider" and a "carrier." Starting Positions: Players should start in the shallow end of the pool, where the water is deep enough for safety but not too deep for participants to stand comfortably. Grip: The rider sits on the shoulders of the carrier and holds onto their partner's neck or shoulders for stability. Gameplay: Objective: The main goal is to knock the opposing team's rider off their carrier's shoulders and into the water. No Hands: Players are not allowed to use their hands to push or grab opponents. The only contact allowed is with the shoulders and body. Legal Moves: Players can use their bodies to push, shove, or attempt to unbalance the opposing team's rider. Illegal Moves: Deliberate grabbing, wrestling, or using hands to interfere with opponents is not allowed. Safety: Emphasize safety. Players should be cautious not to push opponents too aggressively, and carriers should be mindful not to throw riders forcefully. Winning: The team that successfully knocks the other team's rider into the water wins the round. Rotations: Teams can switch roles (rider becomes carrier, and vice versa) after each round to give everyone a chance to experience both roles. Additional Tips: Equalizing Teams: If there is a significant difference in the size or strength of players, consider adjusting teams to make them more balanced. Supervision: Have a responsible adult or lifeguard supervise the game, especially if younger participants are involved. No Rough Play: Encourage friendly competition and discourage overly aggressive or rough play. Game Duration: Set a time limit for each round to keep the game moving and allow everyone to have a turn
