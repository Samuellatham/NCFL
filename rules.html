NCFL RULES

Article 1: Setup

Section 1: Teams

Form teams of two players each. Each team consists of a "Tata" and a "Bunda."

Section 2: Starting Positions

Players should start in the shallow end of the pool, where the water is deep enough for safety but not too deep for participants to stand comfortably.

Section 3: Grip

The Tata sits on the shoulders of the Bunda and holds onto their partner's neck or shoulders for stability.

Article 2: Gameplay

Section 1: Objective

The main goal is to knock the opposing team's Tata off their Bunda's shoulders and into the water.

Section 2: Legal Moves

Players can use their bodies, hands, arms, etc. to push, shove, or attempt to unbalance the opposing team's Tata.

Section 3: Illegal Moves

Pulling of hair and/or any physical contact to sentitive areas, such as eyes, reproductive parts, etc.

Section 4: Safety

Player must stay away from edges of pool. Lifeguards/ Safety Officals must be present. Once Tata falls into water time must be allowed to set back up.

Section 5: Winning

The team that successfully knocks the other team's Tata into the water wins the point.

Section 6: Time

Each game is 30 minutes long, with 15 minute halfs.
